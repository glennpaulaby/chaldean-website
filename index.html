About Us

A Legacy of
Learning Since 1927

Chaldean Syrian Higher Secondary School, Thrissur, stands as one of Kerala's most respected English-medium institutions. Founded in 1927 under the visionary leadership of Mar Abimalek Timotheus Metropolitan, the school has been shaping young minds for nearly a century.

Following the NCERT curriculum, we offer two distinct streams — Science and Commerce — designed to prepare students for the challenges and opportunities of the modern world, while staying firmly grounded in the values of faith, integrity, and excellence.

Our Roots

A Walk Through History

1908

Arrival & First School

Mar Abimalek Timotheus Metropolitan arrives in India and establishes his first school in the Parish of Mar Thoma Shleeha Church, Pattikad — later handed to the government.

1925

Community Resolution

Litigation within the community concludes in April 1925, paving the way for the Metropolitan to establish five new schools.

1927

Foundation of Chaldean Syrian School

Chaldean Syrian School is established in Thrissur as one of the five schools founded by Mar Abimalek Timotheus Metropolitan — beginning a century of academic excellence.

Today

Higher Secondary & Beyond

Now a prestigious Higher Secondary School, led by Principal Dr. Aby Paul, offering Science and Commerce streams to students from across Thrissur and beyond.
